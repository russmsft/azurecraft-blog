Private Endpoints and DNS Without the Drama

Private endpoints are often introduced as a security feature, but the operational challenge is usually DNS. A clean pattern needs clear ownership for zones, links, forwarding, endpoint approvals, and troubleshooting.

What usually goes wrong

  • Workload teams create private endpoints but nobody owns the private DNS zone lifecycle.
  • Zone links are inconsistent across spokes, regions, and environments.
  • On-premises DNS forwarding is treated as an afterthought.
  • Teams cannot tell whether a failure is network routing, DNS resolution, or service configuration.

A useful operating contract

Area Decision to make
Zone ownership Decide whether private DNS zones are platform-owned, workload-owned, or vending-service managed.
Endpoint approval Define who can create, approve, and remove private endpoint connections.
Forwarding Document how Azure, on-premises, and branch clients resolve private names.
Support Give teams a runbook for name resolution, route inspection, and service endpoint state.
Architect's test: if a private endpoint breaks, the support path should identify the owning team before the outage bridge starts.
