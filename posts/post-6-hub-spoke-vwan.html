Hub-Spoke vs Virtual WAN: A Practical Decision

Most Azure network conversations eventually reach the same question: should this platform use a traditional hub-spoke model or Azure Virtual WAN? The right answer depends less on fashion and more on operating model, routing complexity, security inspection, and future scale.

When hub-spoke fits

  • The network team wants direct control over routing, firewall placement, and inspection paths.
  • Most workloads live in a small number of regions.
  • The platform has mature skills for network virtual appliances, route tables, and operational ownership.
  • Custom security patterns matter more than managed connectivity convenience.

When Virtual WAN fits

  • The organization needs managed branch, site-to-site, point-to-site, and inter-region connectivity at scale.
  • Multiple regions or many branches make custom routing hard to operate.
  • The platform team wants a more standardized global connectivity service.
  • Speed of onboarding matters more than bespoke network topology.

Decision questions

Question Why it matters
How many regions and branches are expected? Scale changes the operational burden of custom routing.
Who owns firewall and route operations? Ownership determines whether custom control is useful or risky.
How standard are workload connectivity needs? Highly repeatable connectivity often benefits from managed patterns.
What is the migration path? Existing hub-spoke estates may need phased coexistence rather than a big-bang change.
Practical rule: choose the model the team can operate reliably, not just the one that looks neatest on a diagram.
