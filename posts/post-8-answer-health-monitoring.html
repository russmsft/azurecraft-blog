Answer Health Monitoring for GenAI Workloads

Traditional monitoring tells you whether the service is alive. GenAI monitoring also needs to tell you whether the answers are useful, grounded, safe, and improving.

Two monitoring layers

Layer Typical signals
System health Latency, errors, throughput, dependency failures, token usage, and cost trends.
Answer health Groundedness, relevance, completeness, user corrections, negative feedback, and task success.

Signals worth tracking early

  • Questions with low confidence or missing retrieval context.
  • Answers that users immediately correct or regenerate.
  • Safety events and policy-sensitive prompts.
  • Spikes in token usage after prompt or retrieval changes.
  • Evaluation regressions after model, prompt, data, or orchestration changes.

How to start simply

Begin with a small dashboard that separates uptime and latency from answer quality. Add a review cadence where product, engineering, and risk owners look at failures and decide what should improve next.

Useful framing: production GenAI needs observability for both the machine path and the human outcome.
