Minimum Viable Governance for Azure

Good governance should make the right thing easier. The minimum viable version is not a wall of policy; it is the smallest useful baseline that prevents common mistakes and creates clear ownership.

The baseline

  • Every subscription has a named owner and support path.
  • Allowed regions and high-risk SKUs are controlled by policy.
  • Tags capture owner, environment, application, and cost context.
  • Budgets and alerts are active before spend surprises become escalations.
  • Diagnostics and audit logs are enabled for services that matter.
  • Privileged access has review and expiration expectations.

Keep it usable

Governance fails when teams only experience it as blocked deployments. Pair each control with a documented exception path, a reason, and a support owner. Teams will accept guardrails when they can understand and navigate them.

First review cadence

Review Question
Cost Are budgets and tags telling the story we need?
Access Are privileged roles still needed and time-bound?
Policy Which controls are preventing mistakes, and which are causing friction?
Risk Which workloads need deeper review because of data, exposure, or business criticality?
Practical rule: if a governance control cannot be explained in one sentence, it probably needs a better owner or a better exception path.
