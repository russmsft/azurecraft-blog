AI Workloads Inside an Azure Landing Zone

AI workloads are no longer special cases. Once they move beyond a demo, they need the same platform thinking as any other production workload, plus additional controls for model access, retrieval, safety, evaluation, and cost.

What changes for AI workloads

The baseline landing zone gives you identity, network, policy, observability, and subscription structure. AI workloads add concerns that are easy to miss in early pilots.

  • Model and deployment access must be explicit and auditable.
  • Retrieval needs trusted sources, freshness checks, and access boundaries.
  • Private connectivity matters when enterprise data is involved.
  • Evaluation and monitoring need to cover answer quality, not only system uptime.
  • Token usage and model choice become part of FinOps design.

Landing zone decisions to make early

Decision Why it matters
Where will AI shared services live? Shared model access, search, gateways, and observability may need platform-level ownership.
How will private endpoints and DNS be handled? AI services often sit behind private access patterns once real enterprise data is used.
Who owns quality after go-live? Production AI needs a named owner for evaluation, feedback, regressions, and risk acceptance.
How will usage be attributed? Teams need cost visibility by application, product, environment, or use case.
Useful rule: treat the AI model as one component in a governed application architecture, not as the whole solution.
